abstract: >-
  We present a computational framework for constructing high-fidelity 3D models of
  informal settlements (favelas) in Rio de Janeiro using multi-modal remote sensing data.
  The pipeline fuses terrestrial LiDAR scans, aerial multispectral imagery, and digital
  terrain models to produce physics-ready 3D meshes of densely built urban environments
  where conventional mapping methods fail. These reconstructions feed directly into
  Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) simulations that model ventilation patterns,
  pollutant dispersion, and airborne pathogen spread at the neighborhood scale,
  producing actionable insights for urban public health interventions.
